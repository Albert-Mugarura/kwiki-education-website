On the Ground

The Kampala Project

A grassroots initiative in Kampala combining hands-on craftsmanship, financial discipline, and emotional resilience to transform the lives of families — one day, one skill, one saving at a time.

Overview

Practical Change at the Community Level

The Kampala Project is where theory meets action. Participants engage in three interconnected activities designed to build income, savings, and emotional strength — giving families in Kampala a real foundation for a better future.

🧺

Hands-on Weaving

Participants produce beautifully crafted bags and baskets for major events and markets. These products attract donations and buyers, directly raising incomes for families — with particular impact on school-going children whose school fees and needs are covered.

With consistent practice, both output quality and income have grown significantly over time, creating a sustainable craft-based livelihood.

📈

Financial Stability Training

Participants commit to saving between UGX 1,000 and UGX 5,000 every day. These small, consistent contributions accumulate into meaningful yearly savings that allow families to:

  • Expand their business projects
  • Budget more effectively
  • Pay school fees without crisis
  • Build an emergency safety net
🌿

Emotional Wellness

Financial growth alone is not enough. Participants receive training in:

  • Emotional stability at home and at work
  • Building healthy customer relationships
  • Strengthening community ties
  • Managing stress and conflict constructively

We believe success grows from strong networks and good relationships — and this training ensures participants have both.

Impact

Why This Approach Works

The three pillars of the Kampala Project reinforce each other — creating a cycle of empowerment rather than dependency.

🔄

Integrated Design

Weaving builds income. Savings training builds stability. Emotional wellness builds resilience. Together, they create lasting change that doesn't fade when the program ends.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family-First Focus

Every element of the project is designed with the whole family in mind — especially children, whose education and well-being are directly linked to their parents' stability.

📊

Measurable Progress

Daily savings, increased craft output, and participant testimonials provide concrete, trackable evidence of growth — both for families and for our partners and donors.
